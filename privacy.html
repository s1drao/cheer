4. Text Messaging Program & Consent

CheerClub offers an optional SMS notification program for people who record cheers ("CheerClub Alerts"). If you opt in by entering your mobile phone number in the notification form displayed after you record a cheer, we will send you: (a) a text message when the cheer you recorded plays during the runner's run; and (b) invitation messages when a runner you previously cheered for schedules a new run and chooses to notify their cheer squad.

  • Consent is optional. Providing your phone number is not a condition of using the Service or of recording cheers.
  • Message frequency varies depending on your friends' running activity.
  • Message and data rates may apply according to your mobile carrier plan.
  • Opting out. Reply STOP to any message to cancel at any time. After you send STOP, we will send a single message confirming your opt-out, and you will receive no further messages unless you opt in again.
  • Help. Reply HELP to any message, or contact sidrao1388@gmail.com.
  • Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

No mobile information will be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Text messaging originator opt-in data and consent will not be shared with, or sold to, any third party. All categories of sharing described in Section 5 exclude text messaging originator opt-in data and consent.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the Service and for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy:

  • Runner account information is retained while the account remains active;
  • Voice cheers and associated names are retained while the related run and accounts remain active;
  • SMS opt-in phone numbers are retained until you opt out (reply STOP) or request deletion; records of consent and opt-out are retained as required for compliance purposes;
  • Technical logs are retained for short periods consistent with security and debugging needs.

When information is no longer needed, we delete or de-identify it.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
